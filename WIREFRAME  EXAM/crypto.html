Cryptocurrencies, often referred to simply as "crypto," represent a revolutionary form of digital currency that operates on a decentralized system, independent of government or banking oversight. Secured by cryptographic techniques, these currencies utilize a distributed public ledger known as blockchain to record and verify transactions, making them nearly impossible to forge or double-spend. The pioneering cryptocurrency, Bitcoin, emerged in 2009, ushering in a new era of digital assets.
